Science behind the App

The focus is on four main skill domains: social, behavioral, verbal and academic.

There are several levels and sub-levels within each skill domain. Each user will be asked questions, beginning with the preliminary level of each level. Based on the data analysis of the user’s answers to the assessment questions, the application will decide the level in which the user begins instruction. The application keeps precise records of the data based on the child’s responses throughout the assessment and instruction.

The main principles of behavior used in this application are positive reinforcement and motivating operations..

Features of Neo Rx

The application offers visual schedules, a tracker and a token economy to track behaviors and
instruction progress. It uses eye-tracking and movement-tracking to teach visual tracking and
motor imitation and to demonstrate actions and social skills.
The ABA principles used in the application include generality, effective technology, applied
conceptually, and a systematic, analytic and behavioral approach.

Assessment and instruction
based on Verbal Behavior Milestones Assessment and Placement Program (VB-MAPP)

Discrete trial training
a structured ABA technique that breaks down skills into small, easily taught components backed by positive reinforcement for desired behavior

Token economy
a system for delivering positive reinforcement to children for engaging in desired behaviors or for completing tasks

Differential reinforcement
Through this, children receive reinforcers, such as social attention, praise, edibles and tokens when they engage in desirable behavior.

Response prompt
any form of physical, verbal or visual guidance to increase correction response, which includes modeling and instruction

Stimulus prompt
an antecedent, such as a question, a presentation of an image, or a written direction that cues a child in order to better increase the probability of emitting the target response.

Understanding Autism

What is Autism?

Autism, also known as autism spectrum disorder (ASD), refers to a wide range of symptoms, including challenges related to social skills, repetitive behavior, speech and nonverbal communication. The ways in which people with autism learn, think and solve problems ranges from highly effective to entirely ineffective.

Indications of autism usually appear by age 2 or 3 for those on the autism spectrum disorder. Some related development delays can appear earlier and are diagnosed as early as 18 months. Research shows that early intervention can greatly improve the chances for positive outcomes later in life for people with autism..

GOLD-STANDARD TREATMENT FOR AUTISM

Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) is a type of therapeutic intervention for people with psychological conditions, which helps improve social, communication and learning skills through reinforcement strategies. ABA therapy is an evidence-based method that is based on the fact that we all learn through the consequences of behavior..

ABA specialists use principles from operant conditioning (the science of learning), which establishes rewards for producing desired behaviors or consequences, based on one’s efforts at mastering new tasks or skills.

In other words, if you want your child to learn how to behave in a certain way (such as sitting still) and you reward them when they do so (such as with a treat), then those behaviors will be more likely to become habits over time

ABA therapy also helps children through prompts and by enabling them to choose the correct answers to questions, or to demonstrate the right behavior and reward them through praise and edibles. This will encourage them to repeat that behavior in the future. These are the little steps that are used to help children to learn the right skills and to constantly improve themselves.
